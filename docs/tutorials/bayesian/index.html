Bayesian Statistics

Bayesian inference from first principles: priors, likelihoods, posteriors, MCMC, and practical model building with Stan and brms

Bayesian methods treat unknown quantities as random variables with probability distributions updated by data. The approach yields direct probability statements about parameters, principled uncertainty for predictions, and a natural framework for combining evidence across studies.

This area develops Bayesian thinking from its axiomatic foundations, through conjugate examples that can be worked by hand, to modern MCMC-based fitting of complex models using rstan and brms. Emphasis is placed on prior choice, posterior diagnostics, and the interpretation of posterior summaries in a research report.

Coverage

  • The Bayesian update: prior, likelihood, posterior, and posterior predictive distribution
  • Conjugate models: beta-binomial, normal-normal, gamma-Poisson
  • Informative, weakly informative, and reference priors; prior-predictive checks
  • Posterior summaries: credible intervals, highest posterior density regions, Bayes factors
  • MCMC methods: Metropolis-Hastings, Gibbs sampling, Hamiltonian Monte Carlo
  • Convergence diagnostics: R-hat, effective sample size, trace plots, divergence checks in Stan
  • Bayesian linear and generalised linear models via brms and rstanarm
  • Bayesian mixed-effects and hierarchical models
  • Bayesian model comparison: WAIC, LOO-CV, Bayes factors
  • Bayesian hypothesis testing and the Savage-Dickey ratio
  • Posterior predictive checks for model criticism

Every model is fit end-to-end: prior specification, sampling, diagnostics, posterior summaries, and manuscript reporting. The tidybayes and bayesplot packages are used for tabulation and visualisation throughout.
