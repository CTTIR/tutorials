Survival Analysis

Time-to-event methods: Kaplan-Meier estimation, log-rank tests, Cox proportional hazards, and competing risks

Survival analysis studies the time until an event of interest occurs – death, disease recurrence, mechanical failure, customer churn. It differs from ordinary regression in a single crucial respect: observations may be censored, meaning the event has not occurred by the end of follow-up, and this partial information must be used without bias.

This area walks through the special definitions survival analysis requires (hazard, survival, cumulative hazard), the non-parametric methods that estimate them (Kaplan-Meier, Nelson-Aalen), and the regression models that relate them to covariates (Cox proportional hazards, accelerated failure time, flexible parametric models).

Topics

  • Censoring types: right, left, interval; informative vs. non-informative censoring
  • The hazard function, cumulative hazard, and survival function, and the mathematical relationships between them
  • Kaplan-Meier estimation with pointwise and simultaneous confidence bands
  • The log-rank test and its weighted variants (Peto-Peto, Tarone-Ware, Fleming-Harrington)
  • Cox proportional hazards regression: partial likelihood, hazard ratios, interpretation
  • Checking proportional hazards: Schoenfeld residuals, interaction with time
  • Time-varying covariates and time-dependent coefficients
  • Accelerated failure time (AFT) models with exponential, Weibull, log-normal, log-logistic distributions
  • Flexible parametric models (Royston-Parmar) via restricted cubic splines on the log-hazard
  • Competing risks: cumulative incidence functions, Fine-Gray subdistribution hazards
  • Frailty models for clustered or recurrent event data
  • Landmark analysis and multi-state models

Packages: survival, survminer, cmprsk, flexsurv, rms, riskRegression, mstate.
